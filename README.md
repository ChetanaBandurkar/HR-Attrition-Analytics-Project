# HR-Attrition-Analytics-Project
This project showcases an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard designed using Tableau and Excel to analyze workforce metrics and employee attrition trends. It provides data-driven insights to help HR teams improve employee retention, hiring strategies, and workforce planning.

## üìå Project Objective
-The purpose of this dashboard is to:
-1. Identify key factors influencing employee attrition
-2. Monitor HR performance metrics like headcount and age distribution
-3. Analyze attrition patterns by:
   Department, Age groups, Gender, Education levels & fields, Job roles
4. Support HR in data-driven decision-making to reduce turnover

## üìÅ Dataset Information
- Total Employees: 1,470
- Features include:
   - Demographics (age, gender, education)
   - Employee roles and departments
   - Job satisfaction level
   - Attrition status and reason
   - Performance metrics
    
## Tools & Technologies Used
- Microsoft Excel
  - Data cleaning and preprocessing
  - Data transformation
  - Calculated fields (Attrition Rate, Counts, Age bins)  
- Tableau Desktop ‚Äì Data visualization and dashboard creation

## Analysis Process
- 1. Data Cleaning (Excel)
    - Removed duplicates and null values
    - Standardized categorical fields (Department, Education, Job Role)
    - Created calculated columns for:
       - Attrition Count
       - Attrition Rate
       - Age Groups (bins)

- 2. Data Preparation
    - Aggregated data by:
       - Department
       - Gender
       - Age Group
       - Education Field
       - Job Role
   - Validated totals for employee count and attrition

- 3. Dashboard Development (Tableau)
  - Designed KPI cards for:
     - Total Employees
     - Active Employees
     - Attrition Count
     - Attrition Rate
     - Average Age
   - Built interactive visuals:
      - Department-wise attrition (Pie chart)
      - Age group distribution (Bar chart)
      -  Education field-wise attrition (Horizontal bar)
      - Job satisfaction heatmap by role
      - Attrition rate by gender across age groups (Donut charts)

## Key Insights üîç
- 1. Overall Attrition
        - Total Employees: 1,470
        - Attrition Count: 237
        - Attrition Rate: 16.12%
        - Active Employees: 1,233
        - Average Employee Age: 37
         
- 2. Department-wise Attrition
       - Sales department has the highest attrition (56.12%)
       - R&D follows with 38.82%
       - HR has the lowest attrition (5.06%)
    - üìå Sales roles appear to be the most unstable and require targeted retention strategies.

- 3. Age Group Analysis
       - Highest employee concentration is in the 30‚Äì36 age group
       - Attrition is highest among employees aged 25‚Äì34
       - Attrition significantly decreases after 45 years of age

   - üìå Early and mid-career professionals are more likely to leave.

- 4. Gender-based Attrition
      - Male employees (150) have higher attrition than female employees (87)
      - Across all age groups, males show a consistently higher attrition count

- 5. Education Field-wise Attrition
     - Top fields with highest attrition:
         - Life Sciences ‚Äì 89
         - Medical ‚Äì 63
         - Marketing ‚Äì 35
         - Technical Degree ‚Äì 32
   - üìå Employees from Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds are leaving at higher rates.

- 6. Job Satisfaction Analysis
      - Lower job satisfaction ratings (1 & 2) show higher attrition
      - Sales Executives and Research Scientists have:
      - High employee counts
      - High dissatisfaction levels
      - Higher attrition risk
   - üìå Job satisfaction is strongly correlated with attrition.

## Conclusion üßæ
  - This HR Attrition Analysis reveals that attrition is not random, but strongly influenced by:
       - Department (Sales is most affected)
       - Age group (25‚Äì34 is the most vulnerable)
       - Gender (Male attrition is higher)
       - Education field
       - Job satisfaction levels

 - The dashboard provides actionable insights that can help HR teams:
      - Improve retention strategies
      - Design targeted engagement programs
      - Focus on high-risk roles and demographics

## Business Recommendations üöÄ
 - Improve engagement and incentives in Sales & R&D
 - Implement retention programs for early-career employees
 - Conduct job satisfaction surveys regularly
 - Create customized growth plans for high-attrition education fields
